Comprehensive Guide to Protecting Your Passwords and Online Security

To prevent your passwords from being hacked by social engineering, brute force, or dictionary attack methods, follow these steps:

  • 1. Do not use the same password, security question, and answer for multiple important accounts.
  • 2. Use a password that has at least 16 characters, including numbers, uppercase, lowercase, and special symbols.
  • 3. Avoid using names of family, friends, or pets in passwords.
  • 4. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdates, phone numbers, or postcodes.
  • 5. Do not use dictionary words in your password.
  • 6. Avoid using similar passwords for different accounts, such as "iloveflowersDropbox" and "iloveflowersEmail".
  • 7. Do not store your passwords in your web browser.
  • 8. Avoid logging in to important accounts on public computers or unsecured networks.
  • 9. Use encryption for sensitive information sent over the internet.
  • 10. Use strong encryption methods such as HTTPS or VPNs when traveling.
  • 11. Regularly check your password strength and update weak passwords.
  • 12. Change your passwords every 10 weeks.
  • 13. Use password managers to store and encrypt your passwords securely.
  • 14. Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
  • 15. Avoid storing critical passwords in the cloud.
  • 16. Be cautious when clicking on links in emails or messages for password resets.
  • 17. Ensure your computer is protected with a firewall and antivirus software.
  • 18. Keep your operating system and browsers updated with the latest security patches.
  • 19. Use virtual keyboards to enter passwords on public Wi-Fi to avoid potential sniffing.
  • 20. Encrypt the entire hard drive for sensitive data, and destroy old hard drives if needed.
  • 21. Log in to important websites using incognito or private browsing modes for additional privacy.
  • 22. Use multiple email addresses and phone numbers to protect sensitive accounts.
  • 23. Avoid sharing passwords via email or SMS.
  • 24. Verify software downloads with checksums to ensure they haven't been tampered with.
